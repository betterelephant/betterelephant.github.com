Better Elephant is a community-led campaign seeking to improve the regeneration of the Elephant & Castle.

Our campaign is a response to the recently-revealed masterplan proposals for the area. We feel that the current plans have been shaped according to private interests and not to the benefit of the community.

Our aim is to propose a bottom-up regeneration scheme created by and for the community.

Our proposals are categorised as follows:

  • Housing-> The community purchases and refurbishes the 1,200 empty homes on the Heygate estate from the council under the new ‘Community Right to Buy’ created by the new Localism Act. Read more….

  • Transport-> Removal of the northern roundabout as part of a car-free pedestrian and cyclist friendly development with a 20mph speed limit.Read more…

  • Environment-> Creation of public park within Heygate urban forest. Installation of biomass boiler powering the current district-heating system.Read more…

We believe that the current developer-led plans - which are based on profit maximisation - will result in characterless streets with a bland retail offer, overshadowed by rapidly-aging high-rise blocks. Our vision of a community-led regeneration run by local residents, artists, businesses and community groups, will engage the local community in shaping the space they inhabit, and transform the Elephant into a unique and vibrant creative hub.

The Localism Act, which received Royal Assent on November 15 2011, introduced new rights and powers to allow local communities to shape new developments by coming together to prepare neighbourhood plans. The Elephant Amenity Network has formed a neighbourhood forum and is preparing a neighbourhood plan for the Elephant & Castle based on the Better Elephant proposals.

If you support our plan for regeneration you can sign our petition, or if you would like to get involved - we meet on the 3rd Tuesday of every month at Browning Street Community Hall at 7pm.
