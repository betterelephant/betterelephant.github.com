Socially Sustainable

We do not believe that the current regeneration plans are socially sustainable. Current proposals do not reflect local housing needs at the Elephant & Castle, where there is severe overcrowding and many living in poor housing conditions. The proposed solution to provide more private housing at the high end of the market and reduce the concentration of affordable housing, bears no relation to what local people can afford to pay for their housing and has lead to widespread opposition.

The vast majority of the new private housing is being marketed off plan to overseas buy-to-let investors. This will create a transitory demographic of residents and is not conducive to creating a sustainable community.

The current masterplan also proposes to build only 10% family housing. This is a mechanism for displacement of families and the fragmentation of neighbourhoods.

The recently submitted outline planning application for the Heygate (ref:12/AP/1092) contains no minimum guarantee of affordable housing. The 1200 homes here on the Heygate are truly affordable to local people. The 2500 new homes set to replace them will cost upwards of £500,000 to buy, and if there are any new ‘affordable’ homes built then the most affordable of these are likely to cost in excess of £275 per week to rent.

This regeneration scheme was originally conceived with the aim of creating a more ‘mixed community’ at the Elephant & Castle. In reality what we are seeing is large-scale displacement of those on lower incomes by those on higher incomes. This is also known as gentrification or social engineering and leads only to greater social divide and segregation.

Better Elephant plans to retain and refurbish all of the family homes on the Heygate estate by means of a scheme called ‘homesteading’, which enables people on low incomes to buy and renovate the empty homes themselves.
