'Better Elephant' is a community-led campaign seeking to improve the regeneration of the Elephant & Castle.

Our campaign is a response to the recently-revealed masterplan proposals for the area. We believe that the current developer-led plans - which are based on private interest profit - will result in characterless streets with a bland retail offer, overshadowed by monotonous high-rise blocks.

Our vision of a community-led regeneration run by local residents, artists, businesses and community groups, will engage the local community in shaping the space they inhabit, and transform the Elephant into a unique and vibrant creative hub.

The Localism Act, which received Royal Assent on November 15 2011, introduced new rights and powers to allow local communities to shape new developments by coming together to prepare neighbourhood plans. Better Elephant has formed a neighbourhood forum and is preparing a neighbourhood plan for the Elephant & Castle based on the following proposals.

  • Housing-> The community purchases and refurbishes the 1,200 empty homes on the Heygate estate from Southwark council under the new ‘Community Right to Buy’ scheme created by the new Localism Act. Read more….

  • Transport-> The community develops plans for the removal of the northern roundabout, as part of a car-free pedestrian and cyclist friendly development with a 20mph speed limit.Read more…

  • Environment-> The creation of a public park within the footprint of the Heygate estate, building on the existing E&C Urban Forest campaign. Installation of a biomass boiler powering the current district-heating system.Read more…
