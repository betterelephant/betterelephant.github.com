The Heygate estate in context

“Let us begin by dismissing all the facts.”

Jean-Jacques RousseauFrench Philosopher

Common misconceptions:

  1. The Heygate estate was built on a bombsite. -False, the Heygate replaced a number of victorian tenement buildings, which were not structurally defective but deemed to be slums at the time. (very much like the situation now.)

  2. The Heygate is being demolished because the buildings are defective. __False- the buildings on the estate were declared structurally sound by a council-commissioned survey in 1998.

  3. The Heygate is being demolished because the council didn’t have enough money to maintain the existing buildings. False- The same council-commissioned survey made cost estimates and stated clearly the cost of demolition would be greater than the cost of repair/refurbishment.

  4. The Heygate residents have all been happily re-housed in the area and have the right to return once the new developments have been completed.. False- The ‘Right to return’ agreement expires after 7 years and is not valid for the new homes due to be built on the Heygate footprint.

  5. Some of the new homes replacing the Heygate will be ‘affordable’ and ‘social-rented’. True-, except that Southwark council’s threshold for qualifying for ‘affordable’ housing is a minimum salary of £30,000 and the new social-rented units will only be available under the new 80% of market rent system. This means that a 1-bed flat will cost £275 per week.

  6. The scheme will produce 5,000 new jobs. - This is only a target; similar targets in other regeneration schemes are seldom met, and most of the jobs fail to go to members of the existing community.

  7. The Heygate was blighted by crime and anti-social behaviour. False- This does not correlate with the official crime statistics for the estate. These show a very low level of crime compared to the average crime rate for the borough as a whole.(link)

  8. ‘The loss of 1,260 social-rented flats on the Heygate will be compensated by the construction of new social-rented flats in the area’. False- Only 612 social-rented units at council ‘target rents’ are being built as part of the scheme.Click here for details
