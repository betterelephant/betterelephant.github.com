Common Misconceptions:

  1. The Heygate estate was built on a bombsite. False: the Heygate replaced rows of victorian tenement buildings, which were not structurally defective but deemed to be slums at the time. (very much like the situation now.)

  2. The Heygate is being demolished because the buildings are defective. False: the buildings on the estate were declared structurally sound by a council-commissioned survey in 1998.

  3. The Heygate is being demolished because the buildings contain asbestos. False: The buildings do still contain asbestos, but the harmful types of asbestos were removed during major works in the late 1980s- see 1998 survey. Note that the E&C shopping centre, Metro Heights, Peronnet House etc. all still contain asbestos; as do most buildings constructed throughout the 60s and 70s.

  4. The Heygate is being demolished because the council didn’t have enough money to maintain the existing buildings. False: The same council-commissioned survey made cost estimates and stated clearly the cost of demolition would be greater than the cost of repair/refurbishment.

  5. Former Heygate residents have been given the ‘right to return’ to flats in the new development on the site. True: But the new social-rented flats will be provided under the new 80%-of-market-rent scheme and will cost £275 for a 1-bed flat. Needless to say that only 147 residents are signed up to the ‘right to return’ list.

  6. The scheme will produce 5,000 new jobs. False: This is only a target; similar targets in other regeneration schemes are seldom met, and the final masterplan contains no mechanism to ensure that any jobs created will go to local people, (i.e. min. 30% jobs target for local people).

  7. The Heygate was blighted by crime and anti-social behaviour. False: This does not correlate with the official crime statistics for the estate. These show a very low level of crime compared to the average crime rate for the borough as a whole- see pg. 25 of this 1998 council-commissioned report

  8. ‘The loss of 1,260 social-rented flats on the Heygate will be compensated by the construction of new social-rented flats in the area’. False: Only 612 social-rented units at council ‘target rents’ are due to be built in the E&C opportunity area. Click here for details

  9. The demoliton of the Heygate is being held up by a handful of Leaseholders who are holding out for more money. False: The last remaining leaseholders on the estate are unable to relocate with the low valuations that are being made for the homes.
