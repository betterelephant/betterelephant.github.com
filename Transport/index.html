The Heygate estate in context

Let us begin by dismissing all the facts.

Common misconceptions:

  1. The Heygate estate was built on a bombsite. -no, the Heygate replaced a number of victorian tenement buildings, which were not structurally defective but deemed to be slums at the time. (very much like the situation now.)

  2. The Heygate is being demolished because the buildings are defective.

  3. The Heygate is being demolished because the council didn’t have enough money to maintain the existing buildings.

  4. The Heygate residents have all been happily re-housed in the area and have the right to return once the new developments have been completed.. False- Jerry copy of right to return with expiry clause.

  5. Of the x number of new homes built or due to be built at the elephant only x are social rented accommodation.

  6. The scheme will produce 5,000 new jobs and 4,000 new homes

  7. The development will be zero carbon and sustainable.
