Heygate Estate Redevelopment

Our plans for the regeneration of the Heygate estate include the full-scale demolition and redevelopment of the high-rise blocks. And we are currently exploring the possibility of retaining and refurbishing some of the family maisonette blocks, which are situated inside the forest of 450 mature trees in centre of the estate.

Our plans are looking at ways of retaining the forest of trees and opening up the space with cycle routes and pathways, creating green links from surrounding parks and connecting the rest of Walworth to Bankside and North Southwark.







These proposals are being based on the recommendations made in the 1998 council-commissioned appraisal study. The survey found that the maisonette buildings were in structurally good condition and made recommendations for their retention along with the surrounding green spaces. “It is not only the most cost effective option, but also greatly improves the site environmentally, architecturally and socially.” - (Section 3.2.2)


There have already been a number of imaginative alternative proposals for refurbishing the estate, and altering buildings for alternative use (ie. converting garages to artist studios/workshops etc.). Similar schemes in other locations - like the Park Hill estate in Sheffield have proven to be a resounding success.
